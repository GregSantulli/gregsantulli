N R M L Z

An audio file normalizer.

Audio normalization is the process of amplifying an audio signal to bring the peak amplitude (loudest part) to a target level (here being as loud as possible: -0 decibels). Unlike a compressor, normalization amplifies the signal uniformly, so the dynamic range of the audio is unaffected. This is useful for DJ's and music producers as it is an easy way to bring all audio files to a more consistent volume.
